Augustus Odena

I'm a computer scientist working on Machine Learning. I'm doing a cool new thing that is secret for now but soon won't be. Before that, I was a research scientist on Google Brain.

A lot of my work has been on Program Synthesis -- in which we try to get computers to program themselves. I led Brain's work on Program Synthesis with Large Language Models, co-created Google Sheet's SmartFill Program Synthesizer, and jointly invented the Scratchpad Technique for getting Transformers to perform multi-step reasoning.

Before that, I worked on Image Synthesis Generative Adversarial Networks, though I have also worked on Semi-Supervised Learning and other, miscellaneous topics. Recently, I've been more focused on problems at the intersection of Machine Learning and Computer Systems (e.g. Coverage-Guided Fuzzing of Neural Networks).

Currently I'm a Research Scientist on the at Google, where I used to work in Ian Goodfellow's group and now work in Charles Sutton's group. I was in the first batch of Google Brain Residents, before which I worked at a startup called Nervana Systems.

Before all of that, I was a trader: I worked at this proprietary trading firm and this hedge fund. Before I was a trader, I was a Math major at Columbia University.
